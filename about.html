Our Mandate

Mandate

The mandate of the BURS is to perform tax assessment and collection functions on behalf of the Government and to take appropriate measures to counteract tax evasion on the one hand, and to improve taxpayer service to a much higher level on the other. In fulfilment of its mandate the BURS Act has empowered the BURS to:

  • Administer and enforce the revenue laws;
  • Promote compliance with the revenue laws;
  • Take such measures as may be required to improve service given to taxpayers with view to improving efficiency and maximizing revenue collection;
  • Take such measures such as may be required to counteract tax fraud and other forms of tax evasion;
  • Advise the Minister on matters relating to the administration and collection of tax;
  • And Perform such other functions in relation to tax as the Minister may direct.

BURS Divisions

Communications & Corporate Planning

Communications & Public Relations Office

The Communications and Public Relations Office key role is to create awareness on tax issues and the need to meet obligations and promote compliance amongst taxpayers. The office supports BURS' business areas by compiling material on tax matters and disseminating the information to the public at large and to its internal stakeholders, the employees of BURS. This office also liaises with the media, corporates and other key stakeholders to promote the activities of the Revenue Service. The Communications & PR office is also entrusted with the protection of the image of the revenue service.

Finance & Administration

The Finance and Administration Division (FAD) is responsible for accounting for the revenue collected by BURS according to the various revenue and non-tax statutes and accounting for subventions received from the Government and related expenditure transactions

Human Resources

The Human Resources Division (HRD) is responsible for the management of BURS human resources through applying strategic approaches to recruiting, developing, managing, motivating and gaining the commitment of BURS employees.

Internal Audit & Ethics

The Internal Audit and Ethics Division is an independent appraisal activity established to examine, check, review, appraise and monitor the activities of BURS to ensure effectiveness and compliance with internal control systems and procedures. This is done in order to enhance efficiency, reduce potential risk, protect, and ensure optimal utilisation of assets.

Legal Services

Legal Services Division (LSD)

The Legal Services Division (LSD) plays a dual role in the administration of the BURS Act and the Revenue Laws. The Secretary to the Board is responsible under the BURS Act for the legal affairs of the Revenue Service and accordingly heads the LSD. The LSD is responsible for advising and assisting the Commissioner General on legal matters which arise under the BURS Act, the Revenue Laws and other legislation impacting on BURS`s activities. LSD represents the Commissioner General in litigation matters, particularly revenue court appeals.

Frequently Asked Questions

General FAQ

What does BURS stand for?

Botswana Unified Revenue Service.

Who heads BURS?

BURS is headed by a Commissioner General.

When was BURS established?

BURS was established by the BURS Act of 2003 and came into operation in August 2004.

Why was BURS formed?

To unify and strengthen revenue collection and administration in Botswana.

VAT FAQ

Who must register for VAT?

Any organisation whose taxable supplies exceed P500,000 per annum. Those below the threshold may apply voluntarily.

What is the difference between Category A, B, and C?

Category A & B: Turnover P500,000 up to P12 million. Category C: P12 million and above.

Income Tax FAQ

What period is a tax year?

A tax year runs from 1st July to 30th June of the succeeding year.

Who pays tax on their salary?

Any individual earning more than P3,000 per month (P36,000 per annum) is liable to pay tax.

Customs & Excise FAQ

Where is BURS Headquarters?

Old Lobatse Road, Plot 53976, Old Industrial Sites, adjacent to BOCCIM House.

What is Customs value?

Customs value is the value used for the purpose of levying ad valorem duties – that is, duties expressed as a percentage of the value of goods.
